## How Badger Uses This
- **Matching:** When a task needs a specific skill, Badger prioritises people with that skill who have capacity.
- **Capacity Math:** Daily hours allocated to a person across all active tasks are measured against their Default Daily Productive Capacity.
- **Communication:** Badger addresses people by first name and uses the correct Slack handle for DMs / @mentions.
- **Workload Views:** The dashboard shows % utilised per person per week and flags anyone consistently over 85-90% or with sudden spikes.
- **Holidays/Changes:** When Notes record leave, Badger de-allocates or warns on the affected dates.

## Maintenance Ritual
- Review/update weekly or whenever headcount changes.
- Before a big kickoff, check current utilisation and flag if new work breaks the balance.
- Quarterly: revisit capacities and skills as people grow or roles evolve.

**Note on capacity:** Everyone is currently set to 7h of realistic focused/productive time per day (the full working day minus typical meetings and admin). Adjust per person as needed — e.g. lower for heavy-meeting roles or part-timers, and record leave in Notes so Badger de-allocates those dates.
